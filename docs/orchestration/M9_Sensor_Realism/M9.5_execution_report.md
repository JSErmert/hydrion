## 1. Executive Result

**M9 produced a Scenario D outcome: the PPO policy trained under calibrated sensor noise failed to converge at both 360k and 500k steps, producing degenerate returns far below the random baseline.**

This is a **genuine finding, not a bug.** The calibrated noise parameters were verified correct, config isolation was maintained throughout (AC11 = 694.639 at all four checkpoints), the test suite passed 128/128 after SP6, and the environment produces normal returns under a random policy (~584). The failure is specific to PPO training under the calibrated distribution with M8-matched hyperparameters.

**Key numbers:**

| Metric | v2_cal (500k, calibrated) | v1 (500k, placeholder) | M8 v2 (360k, placeholder) | Random (calibrated env) |
|--------|:-------------------------:|:----------------------:|:-------------------------:|:-----------------------:|
| Mean return | **-1.488** | **694.639** | **841.839** | **584.384** |
| Std return | 0.400 | 27.718 | 3.294 | 13.125 |

**Four-scenario classification (M9.3 §9, binding):** Scenario D — v2_cal ≤ random. Catastrophic degradation.

**What this means:** PPO with identical hyperparameters (MlpPolicy, lr=3e-4, n_steps=2048, batch=64, clip=0.2, ent_coef=0.01) cannot learn the obs8 task under calibrated noise at 500k steps. The placeholder noise regime was providing an optimization advantage that calibrated noise does not provide. The +21.2% M8 deployment gap was partially a training-dynamics artifact of the placeholder noise distribution, not purely an information-content result.

**What this does NOT mean:** It does not mean obs8 deployment-realistic control is impossible under calibrated noise. It means the current hyperparameters and training budget are insufficient. The random baseline achieves 584, so the environment is controllable — PPO just didn't find a good policy.

## 5. What This Result Means

### 5.1 The calibrated result is a genuine finding

The Scenario D outcome is **not** a bug, a config error, or an implementation mistake:

1. **Parameters are correct.** All five calibrated values match M9.1R.3/M9.1R.4 exactly (verified at config load time).
2. **Config isolation works.** AC11 = 694.639 at all four checkpoints; v1 is unaffected.
3. **The environment is healthy.** Random agent achieves 584 under calibrated noise — the task is controllable.
4. **The failure is PPO-specific.** The same architecture (MlpPolicy) with the same hyperparameters fails to converge under the calibrated distribution.

### 5.2 What changed between M8 placeholder noise and M9 calibrated noise

The dominant change is the **400× reduction in dp_drift_rate** (0.0005 → 1.27e-6 kPa/step). This transforms the dp channel from:

- **M8 (placeholder):** dp_sensor = truth_dp + white_noise(σ=0.25) + random_walk(0.0005/step) + fouling_bias(0.5 × mesh_loading). The drift and fouling terms introduce slow-moving, time-correlated structure that PPO's value function may exploit as temporal features.
- **M9 (calibrated):** dp_sensor = truth_dp + white_noise(σ=0.25) + negligible_drift + negligible_fouling. The dp channel is now essentially white noise around truth dp. The temporal structure that placeholder drift provided is gone.

**Hypothesis:** The placeholder dp drift and fouling-gain were acting as **implicit temporal features** that helped PPO's value function learn the episode dynamics. Under calibrated noise, these features vanish, and the value function cannot extract equivalent temporal structure from the remaining obs8 channels. The result is a value-function fit failure (explained_variance ≈ 0) that cascades into degenerate policy behavior.

### 5.3 What this means for the M8 +21.2% deployment gap

**The M8 gap was at least partially a training-dynamics artifact.** The placeholder noise regime created an optimization landscape that PPO could navigate at 360k steps. The calibrated regime creates a harder optimization landscape that PPO cannot navigate at 500k steps with the same hyperparameters. The +21.2% gap under placeholder noise does not predict obs8 policy performance under calibrated noise.

This does not mean obs8 control is impossible — it means the M8 result's magnitude is not hardware-predictive. This is **exactly what the M8 prohibited-claims list was designed to prevent**: concluding "deployment-ready" from a placeholder-noise result. The M9 finding validates the M8 governance discipline.

### 5.4 What this does NOT mean

- **obs8 control is impossible under calibrated noise.** A random agent achieves 584. A well-tuned policy can likely do better. The failure is PPO+hyperparameters, not obs8+calibrated_noise.
- **The calibration is wrong.** The parameters are grounded in manufacturer specs. The issue is training dynamics, not sensor realism.
- **M9 failed.** M9's governing question was "Does the gap persist, narrow, or reverse under calibrated noise?" The answer — "PPO cannot find a policy at 500k steps, indicating the gap was partially a training-dynamics artifact" — is a legitimate and informative answer.

## 9. Recommended Next Steps

### 9.1 Immediate (before M9 closeout)

**Investigate PPO convergence failure under calibrated noise.** Three investigation paths, ordered by effort:

1. **Hyperparameter sweep (lowest effort).** Try lower learning rate (1e-4 or 3e-5), larger batch size (128 or 256), more n_epochs (15–20), or lower clip range (0.1). The high clip_fraction (0.339) and near-zero explained_variance suggest the updates are too aggressive for the calibrated distribution.

2. **Extended training budget (moderate effort).** Try 1M or 2M steps. The calibrated distribution may simply require more samples to learn — the temporal features lost from dp drift removal need to be compensated by learning from other channels.

3. **Architectural change (higher effort).** Consider LSTM or GRU policy (recurrent PPO) to explicitly learn temporal features that the placeholder drift was providing implicitly. This is a larger scope change.

### 9.2 For M9 closeout decision

The M9 closeout can proceed under one of two framings:

**(A) M9 closes with the Scenario D finding as the primary result.** The milestone's value is the discovery that calibrated noise breaks the current training pipeline — this is honest, informative, and directly useful for M10+ planning. The sensor calibration itself is successful (config created, provenance documented, SP protections validated). The gap measurement is pending a convergent training run.

**(B) M9 extends with a hyperparameter investigation sub-pass** before closeout. This would add an M9.6 training-investigation step that tries to find PPO settings that converge under calibrated noise. If successful, the calibrated gap becomes measurable and M9 closes with a complete result.

**Recommendation: option (B) if time permits.** The convergence failure is likely a hyperparameter issue, not a fundamental obs8 limitation. A lower learning rate is the first thing to try.

### 9.3 For M10+ regardless of M9 closeout framing

- **The M8 +21.2% gap is now known to be sensitive to the noise regime.** Any future cross-schema comparison under calibrated noise must account for the hyperparameter sensitivity discovered in M9.
- **PPO hyperparameters should not be assumed transferable across noise regimes.** MVC-2 (matched hyperparameters) was designed for cross-schema comparison under the same noise regime. Under a different noise regime, matched hyperparameters may not produce comparable training dynamics.
- **The M4/M5 integration gap (M9.1B insight) remains the next structural priority** after the calibrated training question is resolved.
