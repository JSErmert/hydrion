## Required Analysis Structure

---

### 1. Executive Integration Verdict

One paragraph:

- will the system remain coherent after calibration?
- biggest risk
- biggest expected improvement
- overall integration confidence

---

### 2. Change Propagation Analysis (CRITICAL)

Trace how calibration affects the system:

#### Step-by-step:

1. sensor parameters change
2. sensor_state changes
3. observation (obs8) distribution changes
4. policy input distribution shifts
5. policy behavior changes
6. reward response shifts
7. evaluation metrics change

For each step:
- what changes
- why it changes
- expected magnitude of change
- risk introduced

---

### 3. System Coherence Check

Evaluate whether:

- truth_state and sensor_state remain consistent
- sensor signals remain physically interpretable
- observation schema remains valid
- policy inputs remain meaningful

Identify any mismatches.

---

### 4. Policy Stability Risk

Analyze:

- will current policy degrade under calibrated noise?
- could behavior collapse?
- could gap reverse?
- could learning dynamics change significantly?

---

### 5. Failure Mode Analysis

Identify potential failures:

- noisy sensor causing unstable control
- policy exploiting unrealistic signal patterns
- reward misalignment under new signals
- hidden coupling between subsystems

For each:
- likelihood
- severity
- detectability

---

### 6. Hidden Dependency Detection

Identify:

- subsystems that implicitly depend on placeholder noise
- assumptions that may break under calibration
- places where calibration could have unexpected effects

---

### 7. System Behavior Validity

Answer:

- could system still behave “correctly” but for wrong reasons?
- could calibration expose previously hidden issues?
- does calibration increase or decrease interpretability?

---

### 8. Risk vs Benefit Summary

Compare:

- what risks calibration introduces
- what risks it eliminates

---

### 9. Execution Readiness Assessment

State:

- is system ready for M9 execution?
- what must be watched closely during execution
- what success/failure signals to monitor

---

### 10. Monitoring Strategy (IMPORTANT)

Define:

- what metrics to track during M9 training/eval
- what signals indicate:
  - success
  - degradation
  - instability

---

### 11. Alignment Confirmation

Explicitly state:

- does this analysis confirm M9.1A conclusions?
- does it reinforce sensor calibration as the correct move?
