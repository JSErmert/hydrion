## 5. Interpretation

### Why M9.5 initially failed (Scenario D)

The M8 PPO hyperparameters (lr=3e-4, clip_range=0.2, batch=64, n_epochs=10) were tuned to the placeholder noise distribution. The 400× reduction in `dp_drift_rate` (0.0005 → 1.27e-6 kPa/step) and 25× reduction in `dp_fouling_gain` (0.5 → 0.02) removed slow-moving temporal structure from the dp channel. Under calibrated noise, the dp channel became a white-noise-around-truth signal — qualitatively different from the placeholder regime. The original hyperparameters produced unstable updates (clip_fraction = 0.339, explained_variance = -0.017), and the policy collapsed to degenerate behavior at both 360k and 500k steps.

### Why M9.6 succeeded

Config A reduced the learning rate from 3e-4 to 1e-4, tightened the clip range from 0.2 to 0.1, doubled the batch size (64 → 128), and increased epochs per update (10 → 15). These changes stabilized the optimization: explained_variance rose to 0.497 (value function learning), clip_fraction dropped to 0.237 (controlled updates), and approx_kl dropped to 0.0057 (small, stable policy steps).

### What changed

**Training stability, not physics.** The sensor parameters, reward function, observation schema, environment dynamics, and action space were identical across M9.5 and M9.6. The only difference was PPO hyperparameters. The initial failure was a training-dynamics issue — the calibrated distribution required more conservative optimization — not an obs8 architecture limitation.

**This finding is itself valuable:** PPO hyperparameters are noise-regime-dependent. Transferring hyperparameters across noise regimes without re-tuning can produce convergence failure even when the task is solvable. Future training under different noise conditions (e.g., domain randomization) should not assume M9 hyperparameters will transfer.
