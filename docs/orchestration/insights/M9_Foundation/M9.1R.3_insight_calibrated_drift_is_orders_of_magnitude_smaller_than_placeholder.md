# M9.1R.3 Insight — Calibrated Drift Is Orders of Magnitude Smaller Than Placeholder

## 1. Core Finding

The placeholder `dp_drift_rate_kPa_per_step = 0.0005` produces approximately
0.2 kPa of accumulated drift over a 400-step episode. A real MEMS piezoresistive
DP transducer (±0.5% FS/year on an 80 kPa span) produces approximately
0.0005 kPa of accumulated drift over the same episode — **400 times less**.

The placeholder drift was a **dominant intra-episode noise source** comparable in
magnitude to the white noise floor (σ = 0.25 kPa). The calibrated drift is
**negligible** — it disappears into the white noise. Under calibrated parameters,
the dp channel becomes a much cleaner signal.

## 2. Why This Matters for M8 Interpretation

The M8 obs8 policy (`ppo_hydrienv_v2`, +21.2% deployment gap) was trained on a
dp channel contaminated by physically implausible drift. Three consequences:

1. **The policy may have learned to tolerate or exploit the drift.** If the drift
   acted as a slow-moving bias correlated with `mesh_loading_avg` (via
   `dp_fouling_gain`), the policy could have used it as an implicit fouling
   estimator. Under calibrated near-zero drift, this channel disappears.

2. **The policy's learned value function was fit to a noisier distribution.** Under
   calibrated noise, the dp channel has lower variance. PPO's value function may
   converge faster and to a tighter fit, potentially improving the policy — or it
   may lose a "regularization" effect that the excess noise provided.

3. **The +21.2% gap magnitude is not separable from the drift regime.** Part of the
   gap may reflect the obs8 policy's ability to function despite 400× excess drift
   (demonstrating robustness), or part may reflect the obs14 policy's advantage in
   having physics-truth channels that do not drift. Under calibrated noise, this
   attribution changes.

## 3. What the M9 Calibrated Result Will Reveal

When `ppo_hydrienv_v2_cal` trains under the calibrated drift (1.27e-6 kPa/step
instead of 0.0005), the dp channel becomes dominated by the already-grounded
white noise (σ = 0.25 kPa) rather than by drift. The dp channel effectively
becomes a pure noise-around-truth signal. This is a qualitative change in the
information content of the channel, not just a quantitative one.

The calibrated result will answer: **does the obs8 deployment gap persist when the
dp channel carries only grounded noise and not physically implausible drift?**

## 4. Relation to Prior Insights

- **M9.0 (sensor calibration is decisive):** Confirmed with specific evidence —
  the placeholder drift was not just ungrounded but orders-of-magnitude wrong.
- **M9.1A (top-heavy system):** The sensor layer was even more placeholder-bound
  than the L2 maturity score implied. The drift parameter alone was off by 400×.
- **M9.1C (reward invariance as coherence anchor):** The reward landscape is
  unchanged regardless of this finding. The policy will train against a different
  obs distribution but the same reward surface.
- **M9.1R.1 (scoping pass vs grounding pass):** This is the grounding pass. The
  numeric values are now concrete and the finding is specific.

## 5. Carry-Forward

1. **The 400× drift reduction is the single most impactful parameter change in M9.**
   M9.3 and M9.7 should track whether the training reward curve and the deployment
   gap respond to this change — the dp channel is 1 of 4 sensor channels in obs8,
   but it was carrying the most distortion.

2. **The `dp_fouling_gain` reduction from 0.5 to 0.02 compounds the effect.** Both
   the drift and the fouling-offset were producing slow-moving bias on the dp
   channel. Under calibration, both are effectively removed from episode-timescale
   behavior. The dp channel after M9 is a white-noise observation of true dp —
   qualitatively different from the pre-calibration channel.

3. **Optical noise increases modestly (0.01 → 0.02).** This is a 2× change in the
   opposite direction from dp drift — the turbidity and scatter channels become
   slightly noisier, not cleaner. The net effect on the 4-sensor-channel obs8
   observation is dominated by the dp cleanup, but the optical noise increase is
   a counterweight worth monitoring.
