# M8.8 Insight — Positive Deployment Gap Is Valid but Confound-Bounded

## 1. Title

M8 closed with a positive deployment gap (+21.2%, obs8 v2 > obs14 v1) — a result that
confirms obs8 viability in-simulation but cannot be interpreted as deployment evidence
without resolving three specific confounds.

## 2. Context

Produced during M8.8 closeout pass. Synthesizes the M8 milestone record from M8.1
(refinement) through M8.7 (execution report). The primary result: ppo_hydrienv_v2
(360k steps, obs8_deployment_v1) returned 841.839 mean return vs ppo_hydrienv_v1's
694.639 (500k steps, obs14_v1). All three ACs passed. Scenario classification:
v2 ≥ v1. Formal closeout status: M8 is closed.

## 3. What Was Learned

**A positive deployment gap closes M8 correctly — and changes nothing about the confounds.**

The four-scenario classification table (M8.3 Section 9) treats "v2 ≥ v1" as one of
four equally valid outcomes, each with its own binding must-not-conclude constraint.
The positive direction does not make the result more certain or more deployment-relevant
than a small negative gap would have been — it simply resolves the scenario question
while leaving the confound structure intact.

**Three confounds are carried forward unresolved:**

1. **Placeholder noise (primary confound).** The sensor-derived channels in obs8
   (indices 4–7) are governed by placeholder noise parameters. The favorable obs8 result
   may partially reflect a noise regime that favors the obs8 policy's dimensionality
   advantage rather than genuine sensor signal quality. This cannot be separated from
   information-content effects without calibrated sensor parameters.

2. **Step-budget asymmetry (secondary confound).** v2 ran for 360k steps; v1 ran for
   500k steps. The positive gap is therefore a lower-bound estimate of v2's potential
   at matched budgets, not an apples-to-apples comparison. A 500k-step v2 run would
   clarify whether additional training increases, maintains, or inverts the gap direction.

3. **Single-seed result (tertiary confound).** Both v2 and v1 used seed=42. No
   cross-seed variance estimate exists for either policy. The gap magnitude (+21.2%)
   has no error bar attributable to training stochasticity.

**The dimensional reduction benefit hypothesis is unconfirmed but plausible.**
A lower-dimensional input (8D vs 14D) may present a simpler optimization landscape
for PPO at this training budget — particularly given VecNormalize's per-channel
normalization statistics. This is a structurally plausible explanation for the positive
direction but is not confirmable without a step-matched 500k-step v2 run and an ablation
that holds dimensionality constant while varying channel content.

## 4. Why It Matters

The M8 result closes a question that was genuinely open entering the milestone: does
removing physics truth channels from the HydrionEnv observation collapse policy
performance? The answer is no, at least under current conditions. This is
information-bearing and non-obvious — it was not known before M8 that a 6-channel
removal would leave performance intact.

However, the result is bounded. Without resolving the three confounds above, the
gap magnitude (+21.2%) and its direction cannot be reliably attributed to the
information content of obs8 vs obs14. The result is internally valid within the
current simulation configuration; it is not externally valid for hardware deployment.

The key discipline: a positive gap that closes M8 is no less confound-bounded than
a negative gap would have been. The prohibitions in M8.3 Section 10 are not weakened
by the positive direction. "Deployment-ready" and "deployment gap solved" remain
prohibited regardless of the sign.

## 5. Immediate System Implication

**The primary confound (placeholder sensor noise) is now the most load-bearing
unresolved issue in the HydrOS deployment pathway.**

Before M8, the primary question was whether obs8 policy control was viable at all.
After M8, that question is provisionally answered (yes, in-simulation). The primary
remaining question shifts to: what does the obs8 control look like under calibrated,
hardware-realistic sensor noise? If calibrated noise is significantly different from
the placeholder regime, the gap direction or magnitude could change.

This makes sensor calibration — not reward shaping, not frontend work, not additional
RL tuning — the most strategically impactful next step for the deployment pathway.

## 6. Workflow Implication

**Positive results require the same epistemic discipline as negative results.**

The M8 governance structure (four-scenario table, prohibited claims list, MVC framework,
calibration confound in eval output) was designed to constrain interpretation regardless
of outcome direction. That discipline proved its value: the eval script printed the
"must NOT conclude: deployment-ready" constraint even when v2 exceeded v1.

For future milestones with positive results: the confound structure must be reviewed
with equal or greater care than for negative results. Positive results are more likely
to generate overclaims. The governance artifacts must be written before results are
known — not retroactively softened after a favorable outcome.

**The 360k-step deviation is an acceptable operational reality for autonomous execution.**

The ceiling of 600 seconds (10 minutes) for autonomous bash execution means that
training runs exceeding ~8–9 minutes must be documented as manual steps in implementation
plans. This is a known platform constraint. Future training milestones must either:
(a) use `--steps N` for a proof-of-concept autonomous run + note the full-budget manual
requirement, or (b) specify from the start that training is a manual foreground step with
explicit `! python -m ...` syntax in the execution plan.

## 7. Recommended Carry-Forward Action

1. **Proceed to M9 — sensor calibration.** The primary confound (placeholder noise) is
   the most direct path toward a deployment gap result that can be meaningfully interpreted.
   All obs8 infrastructure exists. M9 needs only grounded sensor parameter values.

2. **Preserve the prohibited-claims discipline.** The M8.3 Section 10 list applies
   indefinitely to all uses of the M8 result. Do not allow future milestone documents
   to implicitly soften these constraints when referencing M8's positive gap.

3. **Consider a step-matched 500k v2 run as optional M9 infrastructure.**
   If M9 involves a new cross-schema comparison, having a 500k-step v2 baseline
   eliminates the step-budget asymmetry confound. This is not required for M9 entry
   but is recommended before any step-count-sensitive interpretation.

4. **Track the dimensional reduction hypothesis.** If M9 produces a calibrated sensor
   v2 policy and the gap remains positive, the step-asymmetry and noise-regime confounds
   will have been partially resolved. At that point, a dedicated ablation holding
   dimensionality constant (e.g., a 14D deployment-realistic schema where physics truth
   is replaced by sensor analogs) would isolate whether the gap is informational or
   dimensional.
