# M8.1R.3 Insight — The Sources Map Defines the Exact Claim-Validity Boundary for M8

## 1. Title

The M8.1R.4 sources map reveals that every major M8 execution constraint either has
direct literature support or is an explicitly named internal decision — and that four
literature-thin areas create non-recoverable gaps that must be acknowledged in every
claim derived from M8 results.

## 2. Context

Produced during M8.1R.3 sources map pass. Maps all 9 peer-reviewed sources used in
M8.1R.2 against specific M8 design decisions. Separates source-derived constraints
from internal HydrOS architecture decisions. Identifies four literature-thin areas
with no published precedent in the HydrOS domain.

Sources classified: 3 PJ (Kaelbling 1998, Lee 2020, Dulac-Arnold 2021),
6 PC (Andrychowicz 2021, Greydanus 2018, Hausknecht 2015, Pinto 2018, Kumar 2021,
Rudin 2022). All 9 are peer-reviewed. No preprints relied upon for M8 design decisions.

## 3. What Was Learned

**Four mandatory constraints for M8.2 are source-grounded:**
1. VecNormalize refit (S2 — Andrychowicz 2021): reusing ppo_hydrienv_v1_vecnorm.pkl
   for obs8_deployment_v1 is invalid by the most rigorous on-policy PPO study available.
2. Matched training conditions (S2): normalization, hyperparameters, ShieldedEnv config
   must match between v1 and v2 for the comparison to be interpretable.
3. Calibration confound naming (S9 — Dulac-Arnold 2021): placeholder sensor noise is a
   separate, named confound from the observation-limitation under study.
4. Cross-schema framing (S5, S7, S8, S9): the comparison is a deployment-gap measurement,
   not a policy improvement comparison.

These are not stylistic preferences. They are conditions without which the M8 result
is methodologically unsound.

**Seven M8 design decisions are source-derived; seven are internal:**
The distinction matters because source-derived decisions can be cited; internal decisions
can only be described as design choices. M8.2 must not present internal decisions
(schema naming, single-seed budget, ShieldedEnv reuse) as if they were literature-mandated.

**Four literature-thin areas create non-recoverable gaps:**
1. **Flow-control RL ablation**: no precedent in membrane filtration or analogous domains.
   All methodology transfers from robotics and gaming — transfer assumption is plausible
   but unstated in any published source.
2. **Fouling-phase inference from actuator history**: whether obs[6–9] + obs[10–13]
   suffice for fouling-cycle inference is untested. M8 generates the first data point.
3. **Ablation validity under placeholder noise**: no source addresses whether RL ablation
   conclusions hold under uncalibrated sensor models. The M8 result is internally valid
   but its predictive value for real deployment is genuinely unknown.
4. **Single-seed ablation**: multi-seed is the gold standard; single-seed is an
   acknowledged simplification with no literature validation for M8's comparison type.

These gaps cannot be closed by additional research at M8's scope. They are structural
limitations that must be named in M8 acceptance criteria and reporting.

## 4. Why It Matters

The sources map serves a different function than the research brief. The research brief
establishes *what to do*. The sources map establishes *what can be claimed about what
was done*. Without traceability, M8 results could be overstated (claiming deployment
readiness or channel-necessity conclusions that the methodology cannot support) or
understated (failing to recognize that the design choices are grounded in strong sources).

The four-constraint / four-gap structure is the claim-validity boundary for M8:
- **Below the boundary**: internal design choices — described but not cited.
- **Within the boundary**: source-grounded constraints — must be followed and cited in M8.2.
- **Above the boundary**: claims the methodology cannot support — explicitly prohibited
  in M8.2 acceptance criteria.
