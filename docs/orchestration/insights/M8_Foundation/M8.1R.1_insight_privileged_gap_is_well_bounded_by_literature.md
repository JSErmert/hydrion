# M8.1R.1 Insight — The obs8_deployment_v1 Design Is Well-Supported by Literature; Its Limits Are Not

## 1. Title

The architectural decisions behind `obs8_deployment_v1` — retaining actuator feedback,
removing physics truth — are directly supported by multiple peer-reviewed sources.
What remains unsupported is any claim about whether the resulting policy will be
sufficient for the HydrionEnv control problem.

## 2. Context

Produced during M8.1R.1 research pass (M8.1R.2 research brief). Research covered:
observation-space ablation methodology, action history in partial-obs RL, per-channel
attribution, and deployment-gap framing across robotics and RL literature.

Sources verified: Kaelbling et al. 1998, Hausknecht & Stone 2015, Pinto et al. 2018,
Lee et al. 2020, Kumar et al. 2021, Andrychowicz et al. 2021, Greydanus et al. 2018,
Dulac-Arnold et al. 2021.

## 3. What Was Learned

**The schema design is solid.** Retaining controller-known actuator commands (indices
6–9) while removing physics truth (indices 0–5) has direct theoretical grounding in
POMDP information state theory (Kaelbling et al. 1998) and is consistent with the
asymmetric actor-critic framework (Pinto et al. 2018) and with practice in legged
locomotion sim-to-real work (Lee et al. 2020; Kumar et al. 2021). There is no
published argument for removing controller-known actions from a deployment-realistic
observation space.

**The ablation methodology has known limitations.** Training ablation (train from
scratch without channels) is the strongest available method for RL attribution, but
it measures marginal end-to-end contribution, not absolute necessity. The interpretation
of performance delta from M8 must be precise: it measures cost under this training
budget and noise regime, not a general claim about the control problem.

**The calibration confound is the biggest open risk.** The literature does not address
ablation validity under placeholder sensor noise. If M8's sensor noise is far from
real sensor behavior, the ablation result may not predict what happens at hardware
deployment. This is domain-specific to HydrOS and has no published precedent.

**The performance gap is informative regardless of outcome.** If `ppo_hydrienv_v2`
achieves near-equivalent return to `ppo_hydrienv_v1`, this supports — but does not
prove — deployment viability (Rudin et al. 2022 shows training compute can close
this gap further). If it degrades significantly, this surfaces the question of
whether more training, distillation (Lee et al. 2020), or adaptation modules
(Kumar et al. 2021) are needed.

## 4. Why It Matters

The M8 ablation could produce either a favorable or unfavorable result. Knowing in
advance what the result can and cannot prove — grounded in literature — prevents
overclaiming in either direction. A favorable result is not proof of deployment
readiness. An unfavorable result is not proof that sensor-based control is impossible.

The literature provides exactly the right framing: the question is not "can the
policy perform well without physics truth?" in the abstract — it is "what is the
performance cost at this training scale, under these noise conditions, with this
observation set?" That is a bounded, honest, answerable question.

## 5. Immediate System Implication

1. Cite Andrychowicz et al. 2021 as the justification for fitting new VecNorm stats
   to obs8_deployment_v1 (cannot reuse ppo_hydrienv_v1_vecnorm.pkl).

   channel boundary (6–9 kept, 0–5 removed).
3. Include an explicit statement of the calibration-noise confound in the acceptance
   criteria interpretation section.
4. Define the comparison as "cross-schema deployment gap measurement" not "policy
   improvement" in the evaluation protocol.

## 6. Workflow Implication

For future HydrOS research briefs:
- The asymmetric actor-critic / privileged-information literature cluster (Pinto 2018,
  Lee 2020, Kumar 2021, Rudin 2022) is now a standing reference set for any HydrOS
  milestone that modifies the observation regime or moves toward deployment realism.
  These do not need to be re-searched from scratch in future milestones.
- The calibration confound is a recurring structural limitation for all M8 results. It
  should be recorded in M8.2 acceptance criteria as a named limitation, not just in
  prose — so it appears explicitly in every AC check.
- Ablation result interpretation rules (Section 2.3 of M8.1R.2) should be carried
  forward as standing HydrOS evaluation policy, not just M8 guidance.
