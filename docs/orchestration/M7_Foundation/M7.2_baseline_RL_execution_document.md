## 1. Executive Objective

M7's first and only objective is to produce the initial canonical HydrionEnv policy artifact
trained under the `obs14_v1` observation schema. The goal is a clean, reproducible baseline:
not a reward redesign, not a robustness expansion, not a deployment exercise. This artifact
establishes the reference point against which all future HydrionEnv policy work will be
measured. It must be produced with a fixed seed, correct obs schema version-lock, and a
complete metadata record that is honest about what the baseline does and does not prove.

The obs14_v1 policy trained in M7 will operate in a mixed information regime: truth-derived
indices (0–9) are privileged simulation-only channels; sensor-derived indices (10–13) are
noisy partial observations. This is not a true deployment-ready policy. The baseline will
depend on truth channels that are unavailable at hardware deployment. This is an accepted
and documented design choice for M7 — deployment-mode obs design is a future phase.

### Research-informed characterization of obs14_v1

obs14_v1 presents a mixed information structure during training:
- Indices 0–9 are truth-derived. In the formal POMDP sense (Kaelbling et al. 1998), these
  are full-state channels. They carry privileged information not available at hardware
  deployment.
- Indices 10–13 are sensor-derived partial observations with noise, drift, and 1-step
  latency on index 13 (`dp_sensor_kPa`). These are the channels that will be present at
  deployment.

The training regime is a standard MDP (the policy observes a full 14D vector each step),
but the deployment scenario is a POMDP (truth channels 0–9 are not available without
sensor estimation). The M7 baseline trains under the MDP assumption and documents the
deployment gap explicitly.

### Known baseline limitations (documented, not blocking)

1. **Calibration-pending noise parameters.** `dp_drift_rate_kPa_per_step = 0.0005`,
   `dp_fouling_gain = 0.5`, `flow_calibration_bias_std = 0.2 L/min` are placeholder values.
   The M7 policy trains under these parameters. Physical calibration updates will shift the
   sensor observation distribution. Domain randomization of sensor parameters
   (per Tobin et al. 2017; Peng et al. 2018) requires calibrated bounds — deferred to
   post-calibration.

2. **1-step dp_sensor latency.** Index 13 reflects `dp_sensor_kPa` from the previous
   timestep. A MLP policy handles this implicitly under the MDP assumption.
   Dulac-Arnold et al. (2021) support implicit 1-step delay handling as acceptable for a
   first baseline. A recurrent architecture (LSTM) is deferred.

3. **Truth channels as privileged training inputs.** Indices 0–9 are not available at
   hardware deployment. The M7 baseline policy will depend on them. Deployment-mode obs
   design (distillation, adaptation module, or sensor-estimated truth channels) is deferred.
   See Loquercio et al. (2021) and Kumar et al. (2021) for post-baseline architectural paths.

## 4. Environment and Schema Scope

### HydrionEnv — training target for M7 baseline

HydrionEnv with obs14_v1 is the only training environment for this phase. It provides the
sensor pipeline (indices 10–13), truth state (indices 0–9), ShieldedEnv safety wrapper, and
reproducible seeding. All three elements are required for a valid first baseline.

### CCE — explicitly excluded

CCE is not modified in M7. ppo_cce_v2 remains the canonical CCE artifact. Any training run
on CCE in M7 is separate and does not constitute the M7 baseline.

### Observation schema

`hydrion/sensors/sensor_fusion.py` is the canonical source for obs14_v1.
The training script must assert `env.observation_space.shape == (14,)` at startup.

## 6. Validation and Evaluation Expectations

### Minimum required outputs

1. **Training convergence record**
   - TensorBoard log under `runs/ppo_hydrienv_v1_<timestamp>/`
   - Episode return curve shows learning signal by 300k–500k steps

2. **Artifact integrity**
   - All three files exist and are non-empty after training
   - Metadata JSON is loadable and contains `obs_schema = "obs14_v1"`

3. **Post-training evaluation** (≥ 5 episodes, seed=100, deterministic=True)
   - Per-episode: return, episode length, safety violations, ShieldedEnv projections
   - Mean ± std of return across eval episodes

4. **Sensor channel diagnostics** (required for obs14_v1, not present in CCE baseline)
   - Log mean values of obs[12] and obs[13] per eval episode
   - Confirm both channels are non-zero and vary across episodes
   - Confirm obs[12] ≠ obs[0] and obs[13] ≠ obs[1] in at least some steps (dual-channel
     distinction is active, not collapsed)

5. **Random-action baseline measurement**
   - Run 5 random-action episodes (same seed=100) and record mean return
   - Required to satisfy AC7 (policy must outperform random)

6. **Schema consistency log**
   - Training script logs `env.observation_space.shape` to stdout before training
   - Eval script verifies loaded model input dimension matches (14,)

### What counts as a valid first baseline

The M7 baseline is valid if:
- Trained ≥ 500k steps without crash
- All three artifacts saved and loadable
- Eval completes without exception
- Mean eval return > random-action baseline
- obs[12] and obs[13] are demonstrably non-zero during eval
- Metadata correctly records `obs_schema = "obs14_v1"` and `deployment_ready = false`

The M7 baseline does NOT require:
- A specific reward threshold
- Outperforming any prior policy (none exists for HydrionEnv obs14_v1)
- Matching CCE reward levels
- Demonstrating sensor noise robustness (calibration-pending)
- Any deployment-mode evaluation

## 9. Risks and Failure Conditions

### Risk 1 — Stale training script produces misnamed artifacts

**Failure mode:** Developer uses `train_ppo.py` or `train_ppo_v15.py`, which saves
`ppo_hydrion_final_12d.*`. These names suggest 12D obs. Any future load attempt
is semantically ambiguous.

**Prevention:** Do not use stale scripts. Create and use `train_ppo_hydrienv_v1.py` only.

---

### Risk 2 — Schema mismatch at model load

**Failure mode:** Policy trained on obs14_v1 (14D) is loaded against an env that
has obs12_v2 (12D), or vice versa. Crashes or silent shape mismatch.

**Prevention:** Training script asserts `shape == (14,)` at startup. Eval script verifies
loaded model input dim matches env obs space before any episodes run.

---

### Risk 3 — VecNormalize statistics mismatch

**Failure mode:** Eval script loads a VecNormalize `.pkl` from a prior 12D obs run.
Statistics were computed over a 12D distribution; applied to 14D obs, crashes or silently
produces corrupted normalized input.

**Prevention:** Artifact naming with `v1` suffix ties the VecNormalize file to this specific
training run. Eval script must load only `ppo_hydrienv_v1_vecnorm.pkl`.

---

### Risk 4 — CCE / HydrionEnv comparison in reports

**Failure mode:** After M7 training, a report compares `ppo_hydrienv_v1` reward against
`ppo_cce_v2` reward numbers and draws conclusions. The comparison is invalid — different
environments, reward functions, obs schemas, physics pipelines.

**Prevention:** Section 5 prohibits this comparison explicitly. Metadata `note` field
states it. Any report must list environment and obs schema for every metric.

---

### Risk 5 — Treating the baseline as deployment-valid

**Failure mode:** M7 baseline artifact is treated as ready for hardware deployment
evaluation, ignoring the documented limitations (calibration-pending noise, truth channels
as privileged inputs, no domain randomization).

**Prevention:** Metadata `deployment_ready = false` is a machine-readable flag. The
`note` field in metadata states the limitations. This execution document explicitly
lists what M7 does not prove.

---

### Risk 6 — Calibration update invalidates the baseline without documentation

**Failure mode:** After M7 training, physical calibration updates `dp_drift_rate` and
`dp_fouling_gain` in `configs/default.yaml`. The M7 baseline was trained under different
noise parameters. If this is not documented, comparisons between pre- and post-calibration
policy runs will be invalid.

**Prevention:** M7 baseline metadata records `noise_params_status: calibration-pending`.
Any calibration update that changes sensor parameters constitutes a new noise regime. Post-
calibration training runs must be explicitly labeled and not compared against M7 baseline
results without controls.

## 10. What M7 Baseline Will and Will Not Prove

### Will prove

- A canonical first HydrionEnv PPO policy can be trained under obs14_v1 without crashing
- The sensor-derived channels (obs[12], obs[13]) are reachable by the policy during training
- The training pipeline (ShieldedEnv, VecNormalize, schema-locked script, metadata JSON)
  works end-to-end for HydrionEnv obs14_v1
- A reproducible reference exists for future HydrionEnv policy comparisons

### Will not prove

- That sensor-derived channels improve policy performance (requires ablation: obs14_v1 vs
  truth-only obs12-equivalent — future experiment)
- That the policy is robust to sensor noise (calibration-pending noise is not calibrated)
- That the policy transfers to hardware (truth channels are not available at deployment;
  no domain randomization has been performed)
- That HydrionEnv performance is comparable to CCE performance (different environment)
- That the reward function is optimal (reward redesign is deferred)
