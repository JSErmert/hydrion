## 1. Executive Objective

M7's first and only objective is to produce the initial canonical HydrionEnv policy artifact
trained under the `obs14_v1` observation schema. The goal is a clean, reproducible baseline —
not a reward redesign, not a robustness expansion, not a deployment exercise. This artifact
establishes the reference point against which all future HydrionEnv policy work will be
measured. It must be produced with a fixed seed, correct obs schema version-lock, and a
complete metadata record. The obs14_v1 policy trained in M7 operates in a mixed information
regime: truth-derived indices (0–9) are privileged simulation-only channels; sensor-derived
indices (10–13) are noisy partial observations. This is not a deployment-ready policy — it
depends on truth channels unavailable at hardware. That is an accepted and documented design
choice for M7; deployment-mode obs design is a future phase. What M7 will prove: that a
canonical HydrionEnv PPO pipeline works end-to-end under obs14_v1, that sensor-derived
channels are reachable during training, and that a reproducible reference exists for future
comparisons. What M7 will not prove: that sensor channels improve policy performance, that
the policy is robust to sensor noise, that it transfers to hardware, or that HydrionEnv
performance is comparable to CCE.

## 4. Environment and Schema Scope

### HydrionEnv — sole training target for M7 baseline

HydrionEnv with obs14_v1 is the only training environment for this phase. It provides the
sensor pipeline (indices 10–13), truth state (indices 0–9), ShieldedEnv safety wrapper, and
reproducible seeding. All three elements are required for a valid first baseline.

### CCE — explicitly excluded

CCE is not modified in M7. ppo_cce_v2 remains the canonical CCE artifact. Any CCE training
run in M7 is a separate activity and does not constitute the M7 HydrionEnv baseline.

### Observation schema

`hydrion/sensors/sensor_fusion.py` is the canonical source for obs14_v1 (as of M6.2B).
The training script must assert `env.observation_space.shape == (14,)` at startup.
The schema is not to be modified in M7.

## 5. Research-Informed Baseline Design Assumptions

Each assumption below is tagged as either **[source-derived]** (grounded in M7.1R literature)
or **[internal decision]** (HydrOS engineering judgment, not mandated by literature).

---

### 5.1 Mixed Truth + Sensor Observation Regime

**Assumption:** obs14_v1 is treated as a standard MDP input during training (full 14D vector
per step). The policy is not constrained to use only sensor-derived channels.

**[source-derived]** Kaelbling, Littman, Cassandra (1998) formalize the distinction between
MDP (full state available) and POMDP (partial observation only). The obs14_v1 training regime
is an MDP by this definition: at each step, the policy receives a complete 14D vector
including all truth channels. The deployment scenario is a POMDP, because truth channels 0–9
will not be available without sensor estimation at hardware. The training-to-deployment gap
must be documented explicitly and is not assumed away.

**[internal decision]** Retaining truth channels (0–9) during M7 training is an HydrOS
architecture decision for the first baseline. It is the simplest path and allows the policy
to learn the task structure before harder partial-observability constraints are imposed.
It is not mandated by POMDP theory.

---

### 5.2 1-Step Sensor Delay Handling

**Assumption:** The 1-step observation delay on `dp_sensor_kPa` (index 13, confirmed in
M6.1 `test_dp_latency_one_step`) is handled implicitly by the MLP policy. No explicit
delay-handling architecture is used.

**[source-derived]** Dulac-Arnold et al. (2021) identify delayed observations as Challenge 7
for real-world RL. They state: "The naïve approach is to treat the problem as an MDP and hope
that the policy learns to cope with the delay implicitly" — and note this approach works
acceptably for short (~1 step) delays, degrading as delay increases.

**[internal decision]** A recurrent architecture (LSTM or GRU) would be more principled for
delay handling but adds training complexity beyond the scope of a first canonical baseline.
LSTM is deferred to post-baseline. The M7 baseline uses an MLP under the standard MDP
assumption — the 1-step delay is implicitly tolerated.

---

### 5.3 Fixed Placeholder Noise vs Domain Randomization

**Assumption:** Sensor noise parameters are fixed at their current placeholder values during
M7 training. No domain randomization of sensor parameters is performed.

Current placeholder values:
- `dp_drift_rate_kPa_per_step = 0.0005`
- `dp_drift_max_kPa = 2.0`
- `dp_fouling_gain = 0.5`
- `flow_calibration_bias_std = 0.2 L/min`

**[source-derived]** Tobin et al. (2017) and Peng et al. (2018) establish that effective
domain randomization requires the real-world operating point to fall within the randomization
range used during training. Without calibrated sensor parameter bounds, no credible
randomization range can be defined. Training under a single uncalibrated noise estimate is
weaker than proper domain randomization and carries a known deployment distribution-shift risk.

**[internal decision]** Calibration-aware domain randomization of sensor parameters is
deferred until physical calibration provides credible bounds. This is a deliberate scope
constraint for M7, not a shortcut. The baseline's purpose is to establish the training
pipeline and a performance reference point. Calibration updates will shift the sensor
observation distribution; the M7 policy will need to be retrained or re-evaluated under
calibrated parameters. This limitation is documented in artifact metadata, not silently
assumed away.

---

### 5.4 Privileged Information Limitation

**Assumption:** obs14_v1 indices 0–9 are privileged training inputs. No teacher-student
distillation or adaptation module is implemented in M7. The M7 baseline policy will depend
on truth channels that are not available at hardware deployment.

**[source-derived]** Loquercio et al. (2021) demonstrate the privileged information training
pattern: a teacher with full-state access distills into a student operating on partial
observations. The teacher-student distillation step is what bridges the privileged-training
to partial-observation deployment gap. Without distillation, the trained policy is
dependent on the privileged inputs.

Kumar et al. (2021) demonstrate the alternative RMA pattern: a base policy trained with
privileged environment parameters, paired with an adaptation module that estimates those
parameters from observation-action history at deployment. This eliminates the privileged
input requirement without distillation.

**[internal decision]** Neither distillation (Loquercio) nor an adaptation module (Kumar)
is implemented in M7. Both approaches add training complexity beyond the scope of a first
canonical baseline. The deployment gap from truth channels 0–9 is documented explicitly and
deferred to a future phase. The M7 policy is not presented as deployment-ready, and the
metadata `deployment_ready = false` records this.

---

### 5.5 Artifact Comparability Rules

**Assumption:** ppo_hydrienv_v1 (HydrionEnv, obs14_v1, 14D) is not cross-comparable to
ppo_cce_v2 (CCE, truth-derived, 12D). VecNormalize statistics from any prior run are not
reusable for M7.

**[source-derived — thin coverage]** No peer-reviewed standard exists for RL observation
schema versioning or artifact comparability across schema changes. The analogy used is
semantic versioning (semver.org): an incompatible interface change requires a new version
label. A policy trained on k-dimensional input cannot be evaluated on j-dimensional (j≠k)
input without modification; any padded or truncated evaluation is out-of-distribution.
VecNormalize statistics are schema-specific (the running mean/variance are computed over
the training obs distribution; a different schema has a different distribution).

**[internal decision]** CCE and HydrionEnv are treated as independent benchmark paths.
They have different physics pipelines, reward functions, operating envelopes, and obs schemas.
Cross-environment comparison requires independent justification beyond schema versioning.
No such justification exists at the M7 stage. Any report citing both ppo_cce_v2 and
ppo_hydrienv_v1 metrics must list environment and obs schema for every metric.

## 7. Validation and Evaluation Expectations

### Minimum required outputs

1. **Training convergence record**
   - TensorBoard log under `runs/ppo_hydrienv_v1_<timestamp>/`
   - Episode return curve shows learning signal by 300k–500k steps

2. **Artifact integrity**
   - All three files exist and are non-empty after training completes
   - Metadata JSON is loadable; contains `obs_schema = "obs14_v1"`

3. **Post-training evaluation** (≥ 5 episodes, seed=100, deterministic=True)
   - Per-episode: return, episode length, safety violations, ShieldedEnv projections
   - Mean ± std of return across eval episodes

4. **Sensor channel diagnostics** (required for obs14_v1; not present in CCE baseline)
   - Log mean values of obs[12] and obs[13] per eval episode
   - Confirm both channels are non-zero and vary across episodes
   - Confirm obs[12] ≠ obs[0] or obs[13] ≠ obs[1] in at least some steps (dual-channel
     distinction active, not collapsed)

5. **Random-action baseline measurement**
   - Run 5 random-action episodes (seed=100) and record mean return
   - Required to satisfy AC7 (policy must outperform random)

6. **Schema consistency log**
   - Training script logs `env.observation_space.shape` to stdout before training begins
   - Eval script verifies loaded model input dimension matches (14,) before running episodes

### What counts as a valid first baseline

Valid if:
- Trained ≥ 500k steps without crash
- All three artifacts saved and loadable
- Eval completes without exception
- Mean eval return > random-action baseline
- obs[12] and obs[13] are non-zero during eval
- Metadata correctly records `obs_schema = "obs14_v1"` and `deployment_ready = false`

Does NOT require:
- A specific reward threshold
- Outperforming any prior policy (none exists for HydrionEnv obs14_v1)
- Matching CCE reward levels
- Demonstrating sensor noise robustness (calibration-pending)
- Deployment-mode evaluation

## 10. Risks and Failure Conditions

### Risk 1 — Stale training script produces misnamed artifacts

**Failure mode:** Developer uses `train_ppo.py` or `train_ppo_v15.py`. These save
`ppo_hydrion_final_12d.*` — a name implying 12D obs. Future load attempts are semantically
ambiguous.

**Prevention:** Do not use stale scripts. Create and use `train_ppo_hydrienv_v1.py` only.

---

### Risk 2 — Schema mismatch at model load

**Failure mode:** Policy trained on obs14_v1 (14D) loaded against an env with obs12_v2
(12D), or vice versa. Crashes or silent shape mismatch.

**Prevention:** Training script asserts `shape == (14,)` at startup. Eval script verifies
loaded model input dimension matches env obs space before any episodes run.

---

### Risk 3 — VecNormalize statistics mismatch

**Failure mode:** Eval loads a VecNormalize `.pkl` from a prior 12D run. Statistics computed
over 12D distribution; applied to 14D obs — crashes or silently corrupts normalized input.

**Prevention:** Artifact naming with `v1` suffix ties the VecNormalize file to this specific
training run. Eval script must load only `ppo_hydrienv_v1_vecnorm.pkl`.

---

### Risk 4 — CCE / HydrionEnv comparison in reports

**Failure mode:** A report compares `ppo_hydrienv_v1` reward against `ppo_cce_v2` reward
and draws conclusions. The comparison is invalid — different environments, reward functions,
obs schemas, physics pipelines.

**Prevention:** Section 6 prohibits this comparison explicitly. Metadata `note` field states
it. Any report must list environment and obs schema for every metric.

---

### Risk 5 — Treating the baseline as deployment-valid

**Failure mode:** M7 baseline artifact is treated as ready for hardware deployment, ignoring
documented limitations (calibration-pending noise, truth channels as privileged inputs, no
domain randomization).

**Prevention:** Metadata `deployment_ready = false` is machine-readable. The `note` field
in metadata states limitations. Section 1 (Executive Objective) lists what M7 does not prove.

---

### Risk 6 — Confusing privileged-training success with deployment realism

**Failure mode:** M7 policy learns the task effectively, and this is interpreted as evidence
that the sensor-derived channels (obs[12], obs[13]) alone are sufficient for policy control.
In reality, the policy exploits truth channels 0–9. Strong M7 performance does not imply
that a sensor-only policy would perform similarly.

**Prevention:** Section 5.4 documents the privileged-information limitation explicitly.
Any claim about obs[12] / obs[13] policy contribution requires a controlled ablation (truth-
only obs vs obs14_v1) — out of scope for M7, required for a future phase.

---

### Risk 7 — Calibration update invalidates the baseline without documentation

**Failure mode:** Post-M7, physical calibration updates `dp_drift_rate` and `dp_fouling_gain`
in `configs/default.yaml`. The M7 baseline was trained under different noise parameters.
If undocumented, pre- and post-calibration comparisons become invalid.

**Prevention:** Metadata records `noise_params_status: calibration-pending`. Any calibration
update constitutes a new noise regime; post-calibration training runs must be explicitly
labeled and not compared against M7 baseline results without controls.
