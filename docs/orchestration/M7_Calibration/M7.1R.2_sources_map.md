### S1 — Kaelbling, Littman, Cassandra (1998) `[J]`

**Full citation:**
Kaelbling, L.P., Littman, M.L., Cassandra, A.R. (1998).
"Planning and Acting in Partially Observable Stochastic Domains."
*Artificial Intelligence* 101(1–2):99–134.
DOI: 10.1016/S0004-3702(98)00023-X

**Why selected:**
Foundational reference for POMDP theory. obs14_v1 exposes both full-state truth channels
and partial-observation sensor channels simultaneously. The formal distinction between MDP
(full state) and POMDP (partial observation) is needed to characterize what obs14_v1
actually provides to the policy.

**M7 design issue informed:**
Understanding what obs14_v1 is — and what the trained policy will and will not know at
deployment.

**Exact conclusion / decision supported:**
When the observation at training time includes truth-state channels (indices 0–9) that
are not available at deployment, the training regime is formally an MDP (full information)
but the deployment regime is a POMDP (partial information). This gap must be acknowledged
in the baseline design documentation, not silently assumed away.

**Usage role:** Background / framing — establishes the formal vocabulary for characterizing
obs14_v1 as a mixed truth/sensor regime.

**Literature coverage note:** POMDP theory is well-developed. This is the authoritative
foundational reference. No gap in coverage for this claim.

### S2 — Dulac-Arnold et al. (2021) `[J]`

**Full citation:**
Dulac-Arnold, G., Levine, N., Mankowitz, D.J., et al. (2021).
"Challenges of real-world reinforcement learning: definitions, benchmarks and analysis."
*Machine Learning* 110(9):2419–2468.
DOI: 10.1007/s10994-021-05961-4
Preprint: arXiv:1904.12901 (2019)

**Why selected:**
This paper formalizes the specific real-world RL deployment challenges most relevant to
HydrionEnv: delayed observations (Challenge 7) and partial observability (Challenge 9).
It is the most systematic treatment of these challenges in the RL literature at time of
writing, and it includes direct guidance on short-delay handling.

**M7 design issue informed:**
1. Whether to use a recurrent architecture (LSTM) to handle the 1-step dp_sensor latency.
2. Understanding the distribution-shift risk from calibration-pending noise parameters.

**Exact conclusion / decision supported:**
1. 1-step observation delay can be handled implicitly by a MLP policy under the standard
   MDP assumption. Explicit delay handling (recurrent architecture or state augmentation
   with history) is recommended for longer delays but is not required for 1-step delays.
   → **M7 design decision:** MLP baseline, implicit delay handling. LSTM deferred.
2. Observation noise distribution shift between training (calibration-pending parameters)
   and deployment (physical device) is a real-world challenge. The magnitude of the shift
   determines deployment degradation.
   → **M7 baseline documentation:** calibration-pending noise is a known, documented
   limitation, not a blocking concern for the baseline training run.

**Usage role:** Baseline design guidance (delay handling); simulation realism rationale
(noise calibration acknowledgment).

**Literature coverage note:** No thin coverage. This is the primary systematic survey for
real-world RL challenges. The specific 1-step delay guidance is stated in the paper.

### S3 — Tobin et al. (2017) `[C]`

**Full citation:**
Tobin, J., Fong, R., Ray, A., Schneider, J., Zaremba, W., Abbeel, P. (2017).
"Domain Randomization for Transferring Deep Neural Networks from Simulation to the
Real World."
*Proc. IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS) 2017*.
arXiv:1703.06907

**Why selected:**
Primary reference for domain randomization as a sim-to-real transfer technique. Directly
relevant to the HydrionEnv sensor parameter calibration situation: the paper establishes
that randomization range must bracket the real-world value, which is not achievable with
uncalibrated parameters.

**M7 design issue informed:**
Whether domain randomization of sensor parameters (noise levels, drift rates) should be
performed in the M7 baseline training.

**Exact conclusion / decision supported:**
Effective domain randomization requires that the real-world operating point falls within
the randomization range. Without calibrated sensor parameter bounds, randomization cannot
be reliably set to cover the real device behavior. Training under a single uncalibrated
noise estimate is not equivalent to domain randomization.
→ **M7 design decision:** Domain randomization of sensor parameters is deferred until
physical calibration provides credible bounds. The M7 baseline uses fixed placeholder
noise parameters. This limitation is documented, not ignored.

**Usage role:** Simulation realism rationale (deferral of domain randomization); baseline
design guidance (calibration dependency).

**Literature coverage note:** No thin coverage. Tobin et al. 2017 is the primary domain
randomization reference. Peng et al. 2018 (S4) corroborates on dynamics parameters.

### S4 — Peng et al. (2018) `[C]`

**Full citation:**
Peng, X.B., Andrychowicz, M., Zaremba, W., Abbeel, P. (2018).
"Sim-to-Real Transfer of Robotic Control with Dynamics Randomization."
*Proc. IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA) 2018*.
arXiv:1710.06537

**Why selected:**
Extends domain randomization from visual to dynamics parameters (mass, friction, actuator
response). Most directly analogous to HydrionEnv's situation: sensor model parameters
(noise levels, drift, fouling gain) are dynamics-like parameters that affect how the
environment's physics appears to the policy.

**M7 design issue informed:**
Same as S3 — deferral of calibration-aware sensor randomization. S4 corroborates S3 and
adds the specific case of physics/dynamics parameter randomization.

**Exact conclusion / decision supported:**
Randomizing physics parameters during training produces policies that generalize to real
system dynamics. The real-world parameter values must fall within the training range for
transfer to succeed.
→ Corroborates the M7 decision to defer sensor parameter randomization until calibration
provides credible bounds.

**Usage role:** Simulation realism rationale (corroboration of S3 for dynamics parameters).

**Literature coverage note:** No thin coverage. S3 and S4 together cover both visual and
dynamics domain randomization.

### S5 — Loquercio et al. (2021) `[J]`

**Full citation:**
Loquercio, A., Kaufmann, E., Ranftl, R., Müller, M., Koltun, V., Scaramuzza, D. (2021).
"Learning high-speed flight in the wild."
*Science Robotics* 6(59), eabg5810.
DOI: 10.1126/scirobotics.abg5810
arXiv:2110.05113

**Why selected:**
Demonstrates the privileged information training pattern — teacher with full depth state
distills into a student that operates on partial (RGB-only) observations. Zero-shot sim-to-
real transfer achieved by simulating realistic sensor noise. Most directly relevant to
obs14_v1's dual truth/sensor channel design.

**M7 design issue informed:**
Whether obs14_v1's truth channels (0–9) constitute a deployment gap, and whether a
teacher-student distillation step is required for the M7 baseline.

**Exact conclusion / decision supported:**
In Loquercio et al., the teacher-student distillation step is what bridges the gap between
privileged training and partial-observation deployment. Without distillation (or an
equivalent mechanism), the student policy is dependent on privileged inputs. The M7
baseline does not implement distillation — it trains on the full obs14_v1 vector including
truth indices. Therefore, the M7 policy will depend on truth channels at deployment.
→ **M7 baseline documentation:** Truth indices 0–9 are privileged training inputs. The M7
baseline does not implement teacher-student distillation. Deployment with partial (sensor-
only) observations requires a future phase.

**Usage role:** Baseline design guidance (characterizing the deployment gap created by
truth channels); background (privileged information training pattern).

**Literature coverage note:** The Loquercio paper's distillation approach is one specific
implementation. Other implementations (RMA, Kumar et al.) are also relevant (see S6).
For HydrionEnv's specific mixed-channel obs, neither paper directly prescribes the approach
used — the obs14_v1 design is an internal architecture decision.

### S6 — Kumar et al. (2021) `[P]`

**Full citation:**
Kumar, A., Fu, Z., Pathak, D., Malik, J. (2021).
"RMA: Rapid Motor Adaptation for Legged Robots."
arXiv:2107.04034
(Presented at Robotics: Science and Systems 2021)

**Classification note:** arXiv preprint; presented at RSS 2021 (peer-reviewed workshop-
style). Not a full journal paper. Included because it is a widely-cited, influential
demonstration of the privileged information / adaptation module pattern, and because it
offers a specific mechanism (adaptation module from state history) that is an alternative
to Loquercio's distillation. Including both provides a more complete picture.

**Why selected:**
Provides a two-stage architecture (base policy with privileged info → adaptation module
estimating privileged variables from history) as an alternative to Loquercio's distillation.
Relevant to whether the M7 baseline should implement a privileged-input estimation step.

**M7 design issue informed:**
Whether an adaptation module (estimating truth channels from sensor history) should be
included in the M7 baseline architecture.

**Exact conclusion / decision supported:**
Kumar et al.'s adaptation module estimates the privileged environment parameters from the
history of observations and actions, then feeds the estimate into the base policy. This
eliminates the privileged input requirement at deployment without distillation. It is an
alternative to the Loquercio approach.
→ **M7 design decision (internal):** Neither distillation (S5) nor an adaptation module
(S6) is implemented in the M7 baseline. Both approaches add training complexity beyond the
scope of a first canonical baseline. Deferred to post-baseline. This decision is internal
(not mandated by the literature, which supports both approaches).

**Usage role:** Background (alternative privileged-info handling approach); baseline design
guidance (scoping decision to defer).

**Literature coverage note:** S5 and S6 together cover the two main approaches for
bridging the privileged-training / partial-deployment gap. Coverage is adequate. No thin
coverage note required.

### S7 — Semantic Versioning Specification `[T]`

**Full reference:**
Preston-Werner, T. Semantic Versioning 2.0.0. semver.org.
Available at: https://semver.org/

**Classification:** Authoritative technical reference (widely adopted software versioning
standard, not a scientific paper).

**Why selected:**
No peer-reviewed standard exists for RL observation schema versioning. Semver provides
the most widely recognized framework for version discipline in software interfaces. The
principle that incompatible interface changes require a major version increment maps
directly to obs schema dimensionality changes.

**M7 design issue informed:**
Whether the obs14_v1 / obs12_v2 incompatibility is documented correctly, and whether the
artifact naming convention and metadata schema-version fields are following a coherent
versioning discipline.

**Exact conclusion / decision supported:**
A change in interface that breaks compatibility (here: dimensionality change from 12 to 14)
requires a new major version label. This is the basis for obs14_v1 being a distinct schema
from obs12_v2, and for treating policies trained under each as non-interchangeable.
→ Corroborates the obs schema versioning discipline already implemented in HydrionEnv.

**Usage role:** Comparability rationale (obs versioning discipline).

**Literature coverage note:** **THIN COVERAGE** — No peer-reviewed RL-specific standard
for obs schema versioning exists. Semver is the best available analogy. The specific
application to RL obs schemas and artifact lineage is an internal HydrOS architecture
decision.

## Internal Architecture Decisions (Not Source-Derived)

The following M7 design decisions are made on internal engineering judgment, not derived
from the above sources. Each is labeled as such in the research brief and execution document.

| Decision | Rationale | Tagged in |
|---|---|---|
| MLP architecture (not LSTM) for baseline | Simplest first baseline; 1-step delay is manageable implicitly | M7.1R.1 §2, M7.2 §5 |
| Fixed placeholder noise (not randomized) for baseline | Calibration not yet available; randomization deferred | M7.1R.1 §3, M7.2 §5 |
| Truth channels retained in M7 baseline (no distillation) | First baseline; distillation adds complexity; deployment gap documented | M7.1R.1 §4, M7.2 §2 |
| obs schema version label format (`obs14_v1`) | Internal naming convention; analogous to semver but not defined by it | M7.1R.1 §5, M7.2 §3 |
| VecNormalize recomputed per schema | Schema-specific distribution; consistent with standard practice | M7.2 §5 |
| CCE and HydrionEnv benchmarks treated as independent | Different env, physics, reward function — not just schema | M7.2 §5 |
| ppo_hydrienv_v1 artifact naming | Internal convention | M7.2 §3 |
| Metadata JSON required fields | Internal traceability standard | M7.2 §3 |
