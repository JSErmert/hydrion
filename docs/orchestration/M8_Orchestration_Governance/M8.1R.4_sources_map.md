## 2. Full Source List

### S1 — Kaelbling, Littman, Cassandra (1998)

**Citation:**
Kaelbling, L.P., Littman, M.L., Cassandra, A.R. (1998). "Planning and Acting in
Partially Observable Stochastic Domains." *Artificial Intelligence* 101(1–2):99–134.

**Type:** `PJ` — Peer-reviewed journal (Artificial Intelligence, Elsevier)

**Why selected:**
Foundational POMDP framework paper. Establishes the information-state definition: the
agent's information state is the joint history of all observations and all actions taken.
Actions are part of the information state because they are self-generated and exactly
known. This is the theoretical basis for including controller-issued actuator commands
in the deployment observation.

**M8 design issues informed:**
- Justification for retaining obs14_v1 indices 6–9 (valve_cmd, pump_cmd, bf_cmd,
  node_voltage_cmd) in obs8_deployment_v1
- Framing of the POMDP structure of HydrionEnv deployment
- Distinction between hidden state (physics truth, indices 0–5) and self-known control
  state (actuator commands, indices 6–9)

---

### S2 — Andrychowicz et al. (2021)

**Citation:**
Andrychowicz, M., Raichuk, A., Stańczyk, P., Orsini, M., Girgin, S., Marinier, R.,
Hussenot, L., Geist, M., Pietquin, O., Michalski, M., Gelly, S., Bachem, O. (2021).
"What Matters In On-Policy Reinforcement Learning? A Large-Scale Empirical Study."
*International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR 2021)*. arXiv:2006.05990.

**Type:** `PC` — Peer-reviewed conference (ICLR 2021)

**Why selected:**
Most systematic published study of what affects on-policy RL (PPO) performance at scale.
Directly relevant to M8 because M8 uses PPO with VecNormalize. Key finding: observation
normalization has a large, consistent effect on policy performance. This is the primary
literature justification for the requirement to fit new VecNormalize statistics to
obs8_deployment_v1 rather than reusing ppo_hydrienv_v1_vecnorm.pkl.

**M8 design issues informed:**
- Mandatory refit of VecNormalize statistics for obs8_deployment_v1
- Ablation methodology: training conditions (normalization, hyperparameters) must be
  matched between ppo_hydrienv_v1 and ppo_hydrienv_v2 for the comparison to be valid
- Background on what factors matter in PPO training

### S3 — Greydanus et al. (2018)

**Citation:**
Greydanus, S., Koul, A., Dodge, J., Fern, A. (2018). "Visualizing and Understanding
Atari Agents." *Proceedings of the 35th International Conference on Machine Learning
(ICML 2018)*, pp. 2877–2886. arXiv:1711.00138.

**Type:** `PC` — Peer-reviewed conference (ICML 2018)

**Why selected:**
Primary source for perturbation-based saliency analysis of RL observation channels.
Established the principle that contribution is often highly localized and non-obvious —
prior intuitions about which channels matter may be wrong. Also supports the caution
that perturbation measures marginal contribution, not absolute importance.

**M8 design issues informed:**
- Foundation for channel-attribution methods
- Caution: M8 ablation measures marginal contribution, not absolute necessity
- Caution: policy may reorganize under reduced observation, not just degrade
- Supports training-based ablation (train from scratch) as preferred over input-zeroing
  for RL attribution

**Applicability note:** Greydanus et al. work with image-based observations (Atari
frames). M8 uses low-dimensional vector observations (14D → 8D). The principle transfers;
the specific saliency method (Jacobian-based) does not directly apply.

### S4 — Hausknecht & Stone (2015)

**Citation:**
Hausknecht, M., Stone, P. (2015). "Deep Recurrent Q-Learning for Partially Observable
MDPs." *AAAI Fall Symposia 2015*. arXiv:1507.06527.

**Type:** `PC` — Peer-reviewed conference (AAAI Fall Symposia 2015)

**Why selected:**
Primary empirical source for action-history inclusion in partial-obs RL. DRQN processes
(observation, action) sequences through an LSTM — the action is explicitly part of the
input at each step. Demonstrates that history of observations and actions forms a
sufficient information state in practice for POMDP game-playing problems.

**M8 design issues informed:**
- Action inclusion in observation is standard practice in partial-obs RL, not an
  exceptional design choice
- Supports including previous-step actuator commands (obs[6–9]) in obs8_deployment_v1
- Background: for non-recurrent MLP (as used in ppo_hydrienv_v2), the single-step
  obs[6–9] is a weaker form of action history than DRQN's sequence, but is consistent
  with the same principle

**Applicability note:** DRQN is DQN-based (discrete actions, game-playing). M8 uses PPO
with continuous actions. The principle (action history as part of the information state)
generalizes; the specific architecture does not.

### S5 — Pinto et al. (2018)

**Citation:**
Pinto, L., Andrychowicz, M., Welinder, P., Zaremba, W., Abbeel, P. (2018).
"Asymmetric Actor Critic for Image-Based Robot Learning." *Robotics: Science and Systems
(RSS 2018)*. arXiv:1710.06542.

**Type:** `PC` — Peer-reviewed conference (RSS 2018)

**Why selected:**
Primary source for the asymmetric actor-critic framework: critic uses privileged state
information during training; actor uses only deployment-available observations. The actor
trained in this setting is deployable without modification. This is the formal
architectural precedent for the obs14_v1 / obs8_deployment_v1 split in M8.

**M8 design issues informed:**
- Formal justification for the channel boundary: obs[0–5] are privileged (critic-only
  in their framework), obs[6–13] are deployment-available (actor input)
- Supports describing obs8_deployment_v1 as the actor's observation space
- Provides precedent for training a policy that has never seen physics truth channels
  and is therefore directly deployable
- M8 is NOT using asymmetric actor-critic (ppo_hydrienv_v2 trains from scratch on
  obs8_deployment_v1 only) — the connection is conceptual, not architectural

---

### S6 — Kumar et al. (2021)

**Citation:**
Kumar, A., Fu, Z., Pathak, D., Malik, J. (2021). "RMA: Rapid Motor Adaptation for
Legged Robots." *Robotics: Science and Systems (RSS 2021)*. arXiv:2107.04034.

**Type:** `PC` — Peer-reviewed conference (RSS 2021)

**Why selected:**
RMA demonstrates that a base policy trained with privileged environment factors can be
followed by an adaptation module trained using only history of proprioceptive
observations and actions — the deployment-realistic analog of obs8_deployment_v1. Key
existence proof: including actuator history in the adaptation module's input is standard
practice and effective.

**M8 design issues informed:**
- Existence proof that deployment-realistic observations including actuator history can
  achieve near-privileged performance
- Supports the argument that training steps matter: RMA's adaptation module trains
  after the base policy, providing more total compute — if M8's ppo_hydrienv_v2 underperforms,
  additional training or adaptation modules are a legitimate M9 response
- Supports retaining obs[6–9] (actuator history) in obs8_deployment_v1

### S7 — Lee et al. (2020)

**Citation:**
Lee, J., Hwangbo, J., Wellhausen, L., Koltun, V., Hutter, M. (2020). "Learning
Quadrupedal Locomotion over Challenging Terrain." *Science Robotics* 5(47), eabc5986.
DOI: 10.1126/scirobotics.abc5986. arXiv:2010.11251.

**Type:** `PJ` — Peer-reviewed journal (Science Robotics)

**Why selected:**
Most direct precedent in the deployment-gap literature for M8's design. Teacher policy
uses privileged terrain information; student policy uses only proprioceptive sensors (the
robot's self-available signals — equivalent to M8's actuator feedback + sensor channels).
Student achieves near-teacher performance via DAgger-based distillation.

**M8 design issues informed:**
- Most direct structural analog: teacher = ppo_hydrienv_v1 (obs14_v1), student =
  ppo_hydrienv_v2 (obs8_deployment_v1)
- Establishes that removing physics-truth channels while keeping self-available signals
  can be viable in physical control systems
- Caution: Lee et al. use distillation (DAgger). M8 trains ppo_hydrienv_v2 from scratch
  — a simpler approach that may produce a larger performance gap. If M8 shows large gap,
  distillation from ppo_hydrienv_v1 is a literature-supported M9 response.
- Supports framing ppo_hydrienv_v1 as "teacher" and ppo_hydrienv_v2 as "student" in
  reporting, with the caveat that M8 doesn't use the teacher-student training mechanism.

---

### S8 — Rudin et al. (2022)

**Citation:**
Rudin, N., Hoeller, D., Reist, P., Hutter, M. (2022). "Learning to Walk in Minutes
Using Massively Parallel Deep Reinforcement Learning." *Conference on Robot Learning
(CoRL 2022)*, Proceedings of Machine Learning Research, vol. 164. arXiv:2109.11978.

**Type:** `PC` — Peer-reviewed conference (CoRL 2022)

**Why selected:**
Demonstrates that policies trained on deployment-available observations can close the
gap to privileged-observation policies when training compute is increased sufficiently.
Directly relevant to interpreting M8 performance degradation: a large gap between
ppo_hydrienv_v2 and ppo_hydrienv_v1 may partly reflect training budget, not fundamental
information insufficiency.

**M8 design issues informed:**
- Named confound: M8's 500k training step budget may not be sufficient for ppo_hydrienv_v2
  to fully exploit obs8_deployment_v1 signals
- Caution against concluding "deployment-realistic control is impossible" from a
  performance gap at 500k steps
- Supports framing M8 results as "performance at this training scale" rather than
  "fundamental information sufficiency"

**Applicability note:** Rudin et al. use massively parallel simulation (thousands of
environments). M8 uses a single-environment DummyVecEnv setup identical to M7. The
compute scale difference is substantial; the principle (training budget affects
deployment-realistic policy quality) generalizes.

### S9 — Dulac-Arnold et al. (2021)

**Citation:**
Dulac-Arnold, G., Levine, N., Mankowitz, D.J., Lu, T., Abdolmaleki, A., Cobbe, K.,
Marber, T., Goroshin, R., Heess, N., Mouret, J.-B. (2021). "Challenges of real-world
reinforcement learning: definitions, benchmarks and analysis." *Machine Learning*
110(9):2419–2468. DOI: 10.1007/s10994-021-05961-4.

**Type:** `PJ` — Peer-reviewed journal (Machine Learning, Springer)

**Why selected:**
Systematic taxonomy of real-world RL challenges. Provides the specific distinction
between observation noise (channels present but degraded) and observation limitations
(channels absent entirely). obs8_deployment_v1 is an "observation limitation" case —
physics truth channels 0–5 are not noisy versions of themselves, they are categorically
absent at deployment. Also provides the general framework for naming confounds in RL
evaluation.

**M8 design issues informed:**
- Correct framing: obs8_deployment_v1 is an "observation limitation" (category
  absent), not an "observation noise" problem (channels present but degraded)
- Requirement to name confounds explicitly in M8 evaluation output
- Background taxonomy for deployment-gap claims and their limits
- Supports the requirement to state: "calibration-pending sensor noise is a separate
  confound from the observation-limitation studied in M8"

## 5. Literature-Thin Areas

### 5.1 Observation ablation in continuous flow-control RL

No published work addresses observation-space ablation in RL-controlled membrane
filtration, microfluidic processes, or analogous continuous fluid-flow systems. All
ablation methodology cited (S2, S3) comes from robotics and game-playing domains. The
transfer assumption — that training-ablation methodology valid in locomotion and Atari
is valid in HydrionEnv flow control — is plausible but untested.

**Implication for M8:** M8 cannot cite literature to validate its ablation methodology
in the HydrionEnv domain. It can only apply established methodology (matched conditions,
named confounds, training ablation preferred) and acknowledge the transfer assumption.

### 5.2 Actuator feedback vs physics truth in fouling-cycle control specifically

No source directly addresses whether actuator command history (valve, pump, backflush,
voltage patterns) carries sufficient information to infer the phase of a membrane fouling
cycle (loading, near-clog, post-backflush recovery). The RMA paper (S6) provides
existence proof that actuator history + proprioceptive sensors can substitute for
privileged environment state in locomotion — but the fouling cycle is structurally
different from terrain adaptation, with distinct timescales, non-stationarity, and
memory requirements.

**Implication for M8:** Whether obs[6–9] + obs[10–13] suffice for fouling-phase
inference is an empirical open question. M8 will generate the first data point.

### 5.3 Ablation validity under placeholder / uncalibrated sensor noise

No published source directly addresses whether RL ablation results remain valid when
sensor noise parameters are calibration-pending placeholders. If real hardware sensors
have substantially different noise profiles, dp_drift_rate, and fouling gain, the
obs8_deployment_v1 policy learned under placeholder noise may not transfer to deployment.
The calibration-pending confound has no precedent in the sources cited.

**Implication for M8:** The M8 result is internally valid (comparing two policies under
the same placeholder noise) but its predictive value for real deployment is bounded by
the uncalibrated sensor model. This must be stated in every claim derived from M8 results.

### 5.4 Single-seed ablation validity

The sources cited support multi-seed evaluation as the gold standard for ablation
comparisons (variance matters for interpreting whether a delta is real). M8 uses a
single seed (42) for both training runs. No source directly validates single-seed
comparison as sufficient — it is an acknowledged simplification.

## 6. Recommended Carry-Forward Usage

### For M8.2 — Execution document

The following constraints from this source map must appear explicitly in M8.2:

1. **VecNorm refit mandate** (from S2): "A new VecNormalize statistics file must be fit
   to obs8_deployment_v1. The ppo_hydrienv_v1_vecnorm.pkl file must not be reused."

2. **Matched training conditions** (from S2): "ppo_hydrienv_v2 must train with identical
   PPO hyperparameters, episode length, ShieldedEnv config, and seed as ppo_hydrienv_v1."

3. **Named calibration confound** (from S9): "M8 evaluation output must include an
   explicit statement that the comparison is confounded by calibration-pending sensor
   noise. AC checks are at face value; deployment generalizability is bounded by this."

4. **Training budget confound** (from S8): "If ppo_hydrienv_v2 shows large performance
   degradation, evaluation output must note that training budget (500k steps) may be a
   contributing factor, not only information loss."

5. **Cross-schema comparison framing** (from S5, S7, S8, S9): "The comparison is labeled
   as a deployment-gap measurement, not a policy improvement comparison."

### For M8 schema design

- obs8_deployment_v1 = indices 6–13. This is source-supported (S1, S4, S5).
- Do not add any physics-truth channel back to obs8_deployment_v1.
- If a secondary obs4_sensor_only comparison is desired, it must be clearly labeled
  as a more restrictive-than-deployment scenario (beyond scope of M8 primary).

### For interpretation of ablation results

| Result | Correct interpretation | Incorrect interpretation |
|--------|----------------------|-------------------------|
| v2 return ≈ v1 return | obs8_deployment_v1 achieves near-equivalent performance at 500k steps under placeholder noise | "Channels 0–5 are unnecessary" or "deployment-ready" |
| v2 return < v1 return (small gap) | Moderate information loss from removing physics truth at this training scale | "Sensor control is insufficient" |
| v2 return < v1 return (large gap) | Large information loss — may reflect training budget, placeholder noise, or genuine information need; warrants further investigation | "Deployment is impossible" |
| v2 return > random | Deployment-realistic policy learns above-chance control | "ppo_hydrienv_v2 is deployment-ready" |

### For future deployment-relevance claims (M9+)

- Any claim about deployment relevance from M8 results must acknowledge: single seed,
  placeholder noise, 500k step budget.
- Distillation from ppo_hydrienv_v1 (S7, Lee et al. 2020) is a literature-supported M9
  option if M8 shows a large gap. The teacher already exists (ppo_hydrienv_v1).
- Calibration-aware sensor realism (replacing placeholder noise params) remains gated on
  sensor calibration data and is not addressed in any M8 source.
- The standing reference set for future observation-regime milestones is S1, S5, S7, S8
  (POMDP + asymmetric AC + teacher-student + training compute). These sources do not need
  to be re-searched for M9+ work that builds on M8's observation regime.
