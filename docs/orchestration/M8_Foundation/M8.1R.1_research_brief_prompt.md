## Research scope

Focus the research on the following:

### 1. Observation-space ablation in reinforcement learning
Research how RL systems are evaluated when observation channels are removed, reduced, or restructured.

Key questions:
- How should observation-space ablation be designed so results are interpretable?
- What makes an ablation result meaningful versus misleading?
- How should performance loss be interpreted when inputs are removed?

### 2. Action history / actuator feedback in partial-observation control
Research whether retaining action history, commanded actuator state, or controller-known actions is methodologically valid in partial-observation RL / POMDP settings.

Key questions:
- Is it legitimate to preserve controller-known actuator signals while removing non-deployment truth channels?
- How do action-history or control-memory signals help partially observed agents?
- What distinctions matter between hidden state and self-known control state?

### 3. Per-channel information value / contribution in RL observations
Research how channel importance, observation contribution, or information value is studied in RL.

Key questions:
- What methods exist for understanding which observation channels matter most?
- Are there accepted ways to interpret the contribution of specific input groups?
- What cautions apply when inferring "importance" from performance change?

### 4. Deployment-gap quantification logic
Research how sim-to-real or deployment-gap reasoning is framed when moving from privileged observations to more realistic observation regimes.

Key questions:
- How should the gap between privileged training and deployment-relevant observations be described?
- What counts as a useful quantification of that gap?
- What claims should be avoided when comparing privileged and reduced-privilege policies?
