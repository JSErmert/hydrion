### Understand

**What does the post-M7 state mean architecturally?**

obs14_v1 classifies indices 0–9 as "truth-derived / privileged." This classification is
partially incorrect:

- Indices 0–5: genuinely privileged — physics quantities that require a running simulation
  model to compute. Not available at hardware deployment under any standard architecture.
- Indices 6–9: controller command feedback — the controller's own previous-step actuator
  commands. These are trivially available at deployment because the controller itself
  issues them. Labeling these as "privileged" is imprecise.

This distinction has direct implications for the ablation design. A "remove all truth
channels" ablation that removes 6–9 alongside 0–5 is more restrictive than a deployed
system would actually be. The deployment-accurate ablation removes only 0–5 (physics
truth) and retains 6–9 (actuator feedback) + 10–13 (sensors).

**What is the true deployment gap?**

The gap between obs14_v1 and a deployment-realistic observation is exactly 6 channels
(indices 0–5). The policy trained on obs14_v1 may be exploiting those 6 physics channels
in ways that would fail silently at hardware deployment. We do not yet know how much
performance those channels contribute.

**Is that the most important uncertainty?**

Yes. Every other M8 candidate (reward shaping, calibration, frontend) either:
- requires resolving this question first (calibration-aware realism only matters after
  we know what channels the deployed policy will actually use), or
- is decoupled from this question (reward shaping, frontend) but provides less strategic
  clarity about deployment readiness.
