## 4. Remaining Risks

| Risk | Severity | Impact | Mitigation path |
|------|:--------:|--------|-----------------|
| **Single-seed variance** | Medium | The +22.0% gap has no formal error bar from training stochasticity. Two independent hyperparameter configs (A: +22.0%, B: +18.2%) provide directional confidence but not a statistical bound. | M10+ multi-seed validation (2–3 additional seeds on calibrated config) |
| **M4 phenomenological physics layer** | High (structural) | Calibrated sensors observe Layer-A phenomenology, not Layer-B (M5 source-grounded) physics. The policy optimizes against M4 capture curves and electrostatics, not RT-1976 or nDEP. | M10+ M5 integration milestone (per M9.1B insight) |
| **No domain randomization** | Medium | The calibrated result is a single point estimate. No robustness envelope over realistic operating ranges. | M10+ DR over calibrated center-point ranges (per M9.1A.5 Priority 4) |
| **Optical gain coefficients ungrounded** | Low–Medium | Turbidity/scatter noise is calibrated but the NTU-to-mass mapping (gain coefficients) is experimental-only. | M10+ bench-scale optical characterization |
| **Camera sensor ungrounded** | Low | No device class committed; placeholder noise preserved. | M10+ if camera class nominated |
| **Hyperparameter transferability** | Low (documented) | PPO hyperparameters are noise-regime-dependent. lr=1e-4/clip=0.1 works for calibrated noise; may not transfer to DR or new physics layers. | Re-tune at each noise-regime change; documented as M9 finding |

## 5. Next Milestone Direction

> **M10 — Physics Realism Enhancement: M5 Integration into the Production Loop**

The next structural priority is integrating the source-grounded M5 physics modules (`hydrion/physics/m5/capture_rt.py`, `m5/dep_ndep.py`, `m5/materials.py`) into the production simulation loop. Currently, the production env runs M4 phenomenological physics while M5 peer-reviewed code sits unused. M10 would:

- Replace M4 power-law capture curves with Rajagopalan–Tien 1976 single-collector efficiency
- Replace M4 pDEP-flavored electrostatics with nDEP-consistent Clausius–Mossotti capture
- Define how existing canonical policies (v1, v2, v2_cal_A) transition to a Layer-B engine
- Produce the first HydrOS policy trained against source-grounded physics + calibrated sensors

This was identified as the next-most-important structural grounding gap by M9.1B §6 and confirmed by M9.2 §8.1.
