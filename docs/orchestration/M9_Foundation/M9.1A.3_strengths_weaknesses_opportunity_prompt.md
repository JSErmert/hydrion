# M9.1A.4 — Strengths and Weaknesses Register

## Purpose

Create a clear, structured map of:

- what HydrOS does exceptionally well
- what is weakest
- what appears mature but is not
- what must be protected

---

## Required structure

### 1. Executive snapshot

- 3–5 bullet summary of system condition
- must include "top-heavy system" concept

---

### 2. Strengths (High Maturity Areas)

For each strength:

- Layer
- Maturity level
- Why it is genuinely strong
- What makes it unusual or advanced
- What must be preserved

Must include:
- governance/orchestration
- policy/control
- benchmark/lineage discipline

---

### 3. Weaknesses (Low Maturity / High Risk Areas)

For each weakness:

- Layer
- Maturity level
- Why it is weak
- What depends on it
- What risk it creates

Must include:
- sensor layer (primary)
- deployment layer
- visual validity limitations

---

### 4. What Looks Mature But Isn't

Explicitly list:

- artifact
- why it looks mature
- what is actually missing
- what confound applies

---

### 5. Protected System Elements

List what must NOT be broken:

- policy lineage
- schema discipline
- governance constraints
- prohibited-claims discipline

---

---

# M9.1A.5 — Opportunity Uplift Priorities

## Purpose

Translate system weaknesses into **ordered, high-leverage improvements**

This is the most important part of this pass.

---

## Required structure

### 1. Executive priority summary

- Top 3 improvement priorities
- Why they matter
- What they unlock

---

### 2. Opportunity Ranking Table

Each entry must include:

- Priority rank
- Target layer
- Improvement description
- Why it is high leverage
- What it unlocks
- What it depends on
- Risk if ignored

Must include:

1. Sensor calibration (primary)
2. Step-budget alignment (secondary)
3. Multi-seed validation (tertiary)
4. Domain randomization (post-calibration)
5. Visual console (post-calibration)

---

### 3. Dependency Graph (Critical)

Explicitly show:

- which improvements depend on others
- which are prerequisites

Must clearly show:

Calibration → enables everything else

---

### 4. Opportunity vs Risk Balance

Explain:

- which improvements reduce risk most
- which improve capability most
- which could mislead if done too early

---

### 5. Sequencing Strategy

Define:

- what must happen first (M9)
- what can happen next (M10)
- what must wait

---

### 6. Visual Validity Impact

For each major opportunity:

- does it improve visual validity?
- does it risk misleading visuals?

---

### 7. System Uplift Definition

Define what "better HydrOS" means after improvements:

- more realistic
- more interpretable
- more trustworthy
- more visually valid

---

- the system is strongest where it is farthest from hardware
- the system is weakest where it touches reality
- improvement is not about adding features, but correcting balance
