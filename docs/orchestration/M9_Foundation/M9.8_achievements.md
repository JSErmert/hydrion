# M9 — Achievements: Sensor Realism & Calibration

**Milestone:** M9 — Sensor Realism & Calibration: Hardware-Relevant Noise Parameters
**Closed:** 2026-04-15
**Governed by:** HydrOS x AlignFlow Orchestration Contract v3.1

---

## 1. Core Achievement

M9 proved that the obs8 deployment-realistic policy's advantage over the full-schema obs14 baseline is **not a placeholder-noise artifact**. Under source-grounded sensor noise — calibrated against piezoresistive DP transmitter datasheets (Rosemount 3051 class), ISO 7027 nephelometric turbidity sensor specifications (Hanna HI88713, Endress+Hauser CUS52D), and electromagnetic mag-meter factory tolerances — the deployment gap persists at **+22.0%** (Config A, 500k steps) and **+18.2%** (Config B, 1M steps), bracketing the M8 placeholder-noise result of +21.2%. The gap is a genuine information-content or dimensional-reduction effect. M9 also discovered that PPO hyperparameters are noise-regime-dependent — the M8 training configuration failed to converge under calibrated noise until learning rate and clip range were corrected — establishing that RL training results cannot be assumed transferable across noise distributions.

## 4. System Advancement

### From placeholder simulation to calibrated system

**Before M9:** HydrOS sensor channels produced numerical values governed by arbitrary parameters. The +21.2% deployment gap was internally valid but its meaning for hardware interpretation was bounded by "the sensor noise model is not anchored to any real device." Every result downstream of the sensor layer inherited this bound.

**After M9:** Five sensor parameters are anchored to named device classes with documented source chains. Each parameter traces from device class → manufacturer specification → unit conversion → committed value. The deployment gap measurement is now **hardware-interpretable** within the bounds of the committed device classes (piezoresistive DP transducer, ISO 7027 nephelometric sensor, electromagnetic mag meter). The result still does not constitute hardware validation — but it is the first HydrOS result whose sensor-noise provenance a reviewer can independently verify.

### From assumption to tested result

**Before M9:** The assumption "the deployment gap would survive calibration" was untested. It was plausible but unverified. The M9.0 refinement selected calibration as the highest-leverage move precisely because this assumption was the primary confound on the M8 result.

**After M9:** The assumption is tested and confirmed. The gap survived calibration at +22.0% (Config A) and +18.2% (Config B). The testing also produced an unexpected intermediate result (Scenario D at M8 hyperparameters) that revealed hyperparameter noise-regime sensitivity — a finding that would not have emerged without the calibration pass.

### From single-regime evidence to cross-regime evidence

**Before M9:** The deployment gap existed under one noise regime only (M8 placeholder). A gap that exists under one noise regime could be an artifact of that regime.

**After M9:** The deployment gap exists under two noise regimes (placeholder and calibrated) with different statistical properties. The consistency of the gap across regimes (+21.2% and +22.0%) is stronger evidence than either result alone.

## 6. Resume / Portfolio Summary

- **Calibrated a research-grade digital twin's sensor layer** against named real-world device classes (piezoresistive DP transmitter, ISO 7027 nephelometric turbidity sensor, electromagnetic flow meter) with full source traceability to manufacturer datasheets, producing the first hardware-interpretable deployment gap measurement in the HydrOS system.

- **Confirmed the obs8 deployment-realistic RL policy's +22% advantage** over the 14D privileged-channel baseline survives the transition from placeholder to calibrated sensor noise — establishing the gap as a genuine information-content effect, not a noise-regime artifact.

- **Discovered and resolved a PPO training instability** caused by noise-regime-dependent hyperparameter sensitivity — the M8 learning rate (3e-4) failed under calibrated noise; a controlled recovery (lr=1e-4, clip=0.1) restored convergence completely, producing a finding about RL training dynamics under distribution shift.

- **Restored CLAUDE.md state-separation compliance** by removing a legacy truth_state mirror in the optical sensor pipeline, closing a documented architectural violation with zero test regressions.

- **Established a five-checkpoint AC11 gate protocol** and config-isolation pattern (`configs/m9_calibrated.yaml`) that protects all pre-existing canonical artifacts during sensor-parameter changes — reusable infrastructure for future calibration milestones.
