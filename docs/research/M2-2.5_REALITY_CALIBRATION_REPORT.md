# Abstract

HydrOS / Hydrion is a research-grade digital twin for a handheld laundry-outflow microplastic extraction system. While architecturally robust, its value depends on its ability to reflect real-world hydraulic, fouling, and maintenance behavior.

This report consolidates the first **reality-grounded calibration framework** for HydrOS.

Findings:

- Strong evidence exists for:
  - hydraulics
  - pressure-drop behavior
  - fouling structure
  - microfiber loading
  - pressure/flow sensing

- Moderate evidence (proxy-supported) exists for:
  - staged fouling decomposition
  - backflush recovery structure
  - optical sensing behavior

- Weak or absent evidence exists for:
  - device-scale electrostatic capture
  - recirculated-effluent backflush performance
  - microfiber-specific optical mapping

Conclusion:

> HydrOS should first calibrate its hydraulic + fouling + maintenance backbone, then replace proxy assumptions through targeted bench experiments before expanding realism outward.

# 1. Introduction

HydrOS is not a generic simulation environment.

It is a **structured digital twin** for a constrained physical system.

It already achieves:
- modular physics separation
- deterministic pipeline ordering
- truth vs sensor state separation
- validation-aware architecture

However, it does not yet achieve:

> **external credibility**

Internal correctness ≠ real-world correctness.

A simulation can:
- converge
- look realistic
- produce stable policies

…and still encode incorrect physics.

HydrOS now crosses the boundary where:
- architecture is sufficient
- but calibration becomes necessary

With calibration:

# 6. Research Findings

## 6.1 Hydraulics

Key insight:

Laundry systems are **source-dominated**, not device-dominated.

HydrOS must:
- absorb variability
- enforce internal constraints

Strong support:

- washer discharge capacity  
- real-time filter monitoring  

Implications:

- flow envelope is valid  
- scenario engine should include bursts  
- bypass is necessary  

Gap:

- real hydrograph structure  

---

## 6.2 Pressure Drop

Key insight:

ΔP is:
- stage-dependent
- geometry-sensitive
- nonlinear under loading

Pleated filters show:
- area contraction under load
- localized deposition
- accelerated pressure rise

Implication:

> Stage 3 must dominate behavior under fouling

Gap:

- exact coefficients per stage  

---

## 6.3 Fouling

Fouling is not a scalar.

It is composed of:

- cake (surface)
- bridging (fibers)
- pore restriction

Fiber-rich systems:
- accelerate clogging
- reduce permeability rapidly

Observed behavior:
- slow start
- nonlinear escalation
- threshold transition

Implication:

> Nonlinear staged model is correct direction

Gap:

- exact weights and rates  

---

## 6.4 Backflush

Backflush is:

- partial
- diminishing
- geometry-dependent

Important realities:

- not all fouling is removable  
- recovery varies by component  
- repeated pulses lose effectiveness  

Implication:

> HydrOS backflush model is structurally correct

Major gap:

- recirculated-effluent efficiency  

---

## 6.5 Microfiber Loading

Strongest evidence domain.

Observed:

- 9.6 → 1240 mg/kg  
- millions of fibers per wash  

Key properties:

- textile-dependent  
- wash-stage-dependent  
- highly variable  

Implication:

- fiber-dominant default is correct  
- scenario variation is required  

---

## 6.6 Sensors

Strong:

- pressure → realistic immediately  
- flow → realistic immediately  

Weak:

- optical mapping  

Reality:

- optical sensing is noisy  
- requires maintenance  
- is chemistry-sensitive  

Implication:

> Sensor realism is asymmetric across modalities  

---

## 6.7 Particles

Hierarchy:

1. fibers (dominant)  
2. fragments (secondary)  

Morphology matters:
- affects clogging
- affects retention

Implication:

> Particle model must influence clogging, not just inputs  

---

## 6.8 Electrostatics

Supported:

- mechanism exists  
- charge behavior matters  

Not supported:

- handheld-scale performance  

Important variables:

- conductivity  
- surfactants  
- pH  
- zeta potential  

Implication:

> Electrostatics is valid but must remain staged
